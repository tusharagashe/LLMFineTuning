====Improved Proposal======
**Revised Proposal**

Respilimab, a humanized monoclonal antibody targeting IL-13, is proposed for treatment of moderate-to-severe eosinophilic asthma.

Mechanistic Rationale:
Respilimab's mechanism of action involves neutralizing IL-13, a key driver of airway inflammation in eosinophilic asthma. By targeting this molecular pathway, Respilimab aims to reduce airway inflammation and improve lung function. The proposed mechanistic PD readout will be assessed via fractional exhaled nitric oxide (FeNO) levels at baseline, 12 weeks, and 24 weeks post-treatment initiation.

Endpoint Justification:
The primary endpoint measures changes in lung function (FEV1) as assessed by spirometry at 12 weeks post-treatment initiation. Secondary endpoints include reduced exacerbations, measured by standardized exacerbation assessment tool (AEAT), and quality of life assessments using the Asthma Quality of Life Questionnaire (AQoL).

Biomarker Strategy:
Respilimab will be stratified based on fractional exhaled nitric oxide (FeNO) levels, enabling more precise dose selection. Biomarker correlation studies will focus on IL-5 and eosinophil count to validate Respilimab's effectiveness.

Safety Monitoring and Mitigation Plans:

* Regular monitoring for eosinophilic asthma exacerbations will be conducted via AEAT.
* Dose adjustments based on serum IL-13 levels, FeNO levels, and patient-reported outcomes (AQoL) will be implemented to minimize the risk of over-exacerbation.
* Comprehensive safety monitoring plan includes regular blood work and clinical evaluations.

Patient-Reported Outcomes:
The Asthma Quality of Life Questionnaire (AQoL), Symptom Checklists (SCS), and standardized exacerbation assessment tool (AEAT) will be used to assess quality of life for patients with moderate-to-severe eosinophilic asthma.

* Extended follow-up period: ≥24 months
